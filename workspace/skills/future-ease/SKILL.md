# Future Ease

Do a little now. Suffer less later.

## Goal

Reduce tomorrow’s friction with a few small actions now. Do not turn it into a giant planning system.

## Use this for

- Before bed, before leaving, or before a deadline
- When something important happens tomorrow
- When the user tends to forget, miss, or scramble late
- When the user wants to make future life easier

## Do not use this for

- Long-range life planning
- High-risk professional preparation
- Full project-management replacement

## Expected input

The user may provide:
- the current moment
- what is happening next
- what they are most likely to forget
- available time, such as 5 or 15 minutes

If details are missing, still give the shortest effective version first.

## Output

Default structure:
1. One-line judgment: what kind of future friction this is
2. One principle: what is worth doing now
3. 2 to 5 low-cost actions

Useful patch types:
- before-sleep prep
- before-leaving check
- three things tomorrow-you will thank you for
- minimum prevention before a deadline

## Writing rules

- Keep it short
- Keep it concrete
- Keep the threshold low
- Do not create anxiety
- Do not overcomplicate simple prep

## Priorities

1. Prefer actions that take about 2 minutes
2. Reduce the most likely failure point first
3. Do not chase full coverage; chase highest value
4. Keep the list short
5. If the user is tired, prefer 1 to 3 actions over a full plan

## Response pattern

Prefer this order:
- 识别：这是什么类型的未来损耗
- 风险：如果现在不补，会在哪个点最麻烦
- 补丁：先做哪些就够
- 可执行动作：给 2 到 5 个现在就能做的动作

## Success standard

Success means:
- the user can act immediately
- tomorrow has one fewer avoidable failure
- the list is short but useful
- the prep itself does not become another burden
