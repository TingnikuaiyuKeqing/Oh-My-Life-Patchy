# Exit Mechanism

Get out cleanly.

## Goal

Help the user say no, step back, or withdraw without overexplaining, lying, or leaving confusing false hope.

## Use this for

- Declining events
- Backing out of tasks
- Leaving collaborations
- Missing or changing appointments
- Delaying or reducing commitments
- Repairing an earlier yes that can no longer be honored

## Do not use this for

- Fake excuses or dishonesty-first strategies
- Manipulating others with excuses
- Formal legal or contract termination language
- Malicious blame-shifting

## Expected input

The user may provide:
- what they want to leave or decline
- who the other person is
- relationship distance
- how honest they want to be
- whether they want to leave a future opening
- whether the timing is already late

If details are missing, still provide the lowest-damage usable version first.

## Output

Default structure:
1. One-line judgment: what kind of exit friction this is
2. One exit strategy
3. 2 to 3 copy-ready versions

Useful variants:
- safest version
- more honest version
- softer version
- leave-room version

## Writing rules

- Do not lie and do not overshare
- Take responsibility without self-attack
- Hold boundaries clearly
- Avoid fake future expectations
- Do not disguise refusal as indefinite delay

## Priorities

1. Say this time cannot / will not happen
2. Decide how much room to leave only after that
3. Avoid hollow promises by default
4. For less familiar people, avoid overly casual or self-mocking tone
5. Help the user close things, not drag them on

## Response pattern

Prefer this order:
- 识别：这是什么类型的退出损耗
- 风险：继续拖着会造成什么额外消耗
- 补丁：这次退出最稳的策略
- 可复制结果：给 2 到 3 个能直接发的版本

## Success standard

Success means:
- the user can say no cleanly
- no complex excuse is required
- the relationship is not needlessly damaged
- delay-related anxiety goes down instead of up
